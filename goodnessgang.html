Goodness Gang Go App Series

A Goodness Gang Collect-a-thon

Known internally as the Goodness Gang Go app series, it is more precisely a series of various app releases for different store partners across several regions: VitaMini's by Lidl in the Netherlands, Gang Świeżaków by Beidronka in Poland, and, more recognizably, Goodness Gang Go by Lidl in Ireland.
Regardless of what it goes by, the aim of the app remains the same: help your avatar find the Goodness Gang characters by finding and scanning special codes! Each new character you find unlocks a fun game to play! Play games to collect points that you can use in the shop to upgrade your garden and character!

Creating the app

The Goodness Gang Go app series was perhaps one of the most ambitious projects of the time. Rather than building one core app translated for different regions, each partner was to receive a different app with a different cast of characters, games, and cosmetic changes to suit their in-store campaigns. In a sense, we were actually building multiple apps with a common infrastructure.
As can probably be guessed from its name, the idea for the Goodness Gang Go app originated from the popularity of Pokemon Go. Similarly to Pokemon, the main aim of the app is to collect all the game characters. Unlike Pokemon Go, however, characters and mini-games were unlocked by scanning codes printed on store promotions and merchandise, ranging from cuddly toys to in-store floor mats depending on the partner and campaign.
The initial concept for Goodness Gang Go involved the user 'upgrading' the Goodness Gang members themselves. We decided to rethink this idea and introduce the concept of an avatar character whose garden and wardrobe could be upgraded by unlocking characters and playing games. The avatars were designed in-house, which in itself was a delightful collaborative effort from everyone.
Although the Goodness Gang are a pre-established franchise, the actual characters vary greatly in design, style, and gender depending on the iteration and market. Our design and 3D team had to collaborate closely with the client to restyle the characters for the app. There is certainly a Disney-esque inspiration in the final characters, one which makes them quite unique and expressive.
Perhaps most challenging of all, the team had the task of creating a large number of mini-games for the user to unlock. We worked closely with the client to propose kid-friendly concepts that could range from frantic fun to educational. In order to evenly balance each games rewards, we created a 'conversion' step of sorts: at the end of a round, the user's game points would enter an animated blender that would turn them into currency points to be used in the main app. We were pleased by the result as it gave more freedom for our game designs.
