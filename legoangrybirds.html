Lego Angry Birds

A Lego Egg Hunt

Following the launch of the first Angry Birds Movie, Lego and Rovio released six Angry Birds-themed playsets. In an effort to further blur the line between physical and digital, Rovio worked alongside Zappar to embed an AR scanner inside their Angry Birds Action App. By scanning specially marked birdcodes, consumers were able to point their devices on packaging and print material to unlock unique AR Lego mini-games.

Each Lego instruction manual brings to life a 3D virtual level of the Lego set. Users must explore the augmented environment and find as many eggs as possible within an alotted period of time. Lego Piggies run abound -- tapping them and knocking them out will force them to drop eggs as well!

At the end of the game, users are scored according to their performance, which subsequently unlocks a unique power-up that they can use in-game in Angry Birds Action! This is a great example where agumented reality was used seemlessly to enhance product engagement!
